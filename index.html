Is Beyoncé Secretly a Rapper?

There is no arguing that Beyoncé is a wildly accomplished singer, songwriter and performer. She's got a hoard of GRAMMYs to prove it, and a hive-full of fans who rightfully adore her. But would you -or anyone you know - call her a rapper? These questions first came to me while listening to Megan Thee Stallion's Savage Remix. I had a real record-scratch moment. How did I breeze by the fact that Beyoncé is really rapping on this song? Is she flying under the radar as a rapper because she is such an accomplished singer?

I set out to investigate:

  1. Is Beyoncé a (low-key) rapper?
  2. Is her rap music more or less popular?

I listened to her entire body of work to make a determination.

How to read: Studio albums are plotted over time. Each ring around an album represents a track which features Beyoncé rapping. Ex. 2 Rings = 2 rap features. Music notes represent singles.

How to interact: Select a metric to compare Beyoncé albums/songs over time. Hover for additional details. Click a track or single to play a 30-second sample (courtesy of Spotify). To stop playback, click again, or use audio controls which will scroll as you do.

Notes: The spotify popularity of a track is a value between 0 and 100, with 100 being the most popular. The popularity is calculated by algorithm and is based on the total number of plays the track has had and how recent those plays are. Album popularity is derived mathematically from track popularity. Percent Rap of the album is found by dividing the number of tracks featuring Beyoncé rapping by the total number of tracks on the album.

Song to Song Comparison

I thought it would be interesting to take this one step further: Is there a difference in popularity of rapped songs vs. non rapped songs? Is it clear that rapped songs dominate any part of Beyoncé's career? I added spotify speechiness* as a metric to see which songs fall into the rap realm according to Spotify. Examining both metrics might make it clearer which songs dominate which parts of Beyoncé's career.

Note: Spotify speechiness detects the presence of spoken words in a track. The more exclusively speech-like the recording (e.g. talk show, audio book, poetry), the closer to 1.0 the attribute value. Values above 0.66 describe tracks that are probably made entirely of spoken words. Values between 0.33 (Rap Threshold) and 0.66 describe tracks that may contain both music and speech, either in sections or layered, including such cases as rap music. Values below 0.33 most likely represent music and other non-speech-like tracks.

Another Way to Determine Popularity

Spotify popularity takes recency of a stream into account. Theoretically a more recent album will be higher ranked in popularity. While that ultimately shows whether music stands the test of time, I was curious to see if how my questions would be answered using another metric for popularity. So I looked into Billboard Hot 100 data. I asked the same questions: Is Beyoncé a rapper? Are rap songs more or less popular?

Beyoncé is THE Bar

Beyoncé is clearly rapping with increasing frequency right under our noses. So when someone asks you who your favorite rapper is, you officially have permission to name Beyoncé and show the receipts.

As for the question of popularity, I expected to see an inverse relationship between rap content and popularity, but the reality is more nuanced than that. On the Billboard Charts and at the GRAMMYs Beyoncé seems to be slightly less celebrated as a rapper. RENAISSANCE act I seems to be universally popular. The ultimate conclusion is that we have enjoyed 20 years of Beyoncé's contributions to music and she keeps raising the bar. Whether it's with her gorgeous singing voice or these sneaky BARS.

No matter what we hear from the Queen next, we're lucky to be living in a time when Beyoncé is Beyoncé-ing this hard.
